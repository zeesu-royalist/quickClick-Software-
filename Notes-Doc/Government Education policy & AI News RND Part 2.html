Budget 2024-25 Union Budget July 2024

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman presented the first budget of the Modi 3.0 government on July 23, 2024. A total of ₹1.48 lakh crore was allocated for education, employment, and skilling together. Key education highlights included:

  • Financial support for domestic education loans up to ₹10 lakh for students in higher education
  • E-vouchers to be issued to 1 lakh students annually for a 3% interest subsidy on education loans
  • Upgradation of 1,000 Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) with industry-aligned courses
  • Internship scheme for 1 crore youth in top 500 companies, with a monthly stipend of ₹5,000
  • Training of 20 lakh youth over five years in partnership with states and industries
  • School education allocated ₹73,008 crore; ₹4,200 crore earmarked for the PM SHRI scheme
Source: investindia.gov.in – Budget 2024-25 Key Highlights
Budget 2025-26 Union Budget February 2025

The 2025-26 budget marked a major step towards AI-driven education and STEM infrastructure for schools. Total education outlay reached ₹1.28 lakh crore. Key announcements:

  • ₹500 crore for Centre of Excellence (CoE) in AI for Education — the 4th AI CoE in India
  • ₹2,000 crore for the IndiaAI Mission (total outlay ₹10,372 crore over 5 years)
  • 50,000 Atal Tinkering Labs to be set up in government schools over 5 years
  • ATL budget increased by 300%, from ₹96.83 crore to ₹400 crore
  • Broadband connectivity (BharatNet) to all government secondary schools in rural areas
  • IIT seat expansion: 6,500 new seats added; IITs to offer AI, Robotics, Biotechnology courses
  • PM Research Fellowship expanded to 10,000 fellows for research at IITs and IISc
  • ₹20,000 crore for private sector R&D over 5 years
  • Five National Centres of Excellence for Skilling, set up with global partnerships
  • Bharatiya Bhasha Pustak Scheme for digital-form Indian language textbooks for school students
Source: indiaai.gov.in – Budget 2025-26: AI Centre of Excellence
Budget 2026-27 Union Budget February 2026 — Highest-Ever Allocation

Finance Minister Sitharaman presented the Budget 2026-27 on 1 February 2026, announcing a record ₹1,39,289 crore for education — an 8.27% increase from the previous year. School education alone received ₹83,562 crore. Key highlights:

  • AVGC Content Creator Labs in 15,000 secondary schools and 500 colleges (Animation, Visual Effects, Gaming, Comics)
  • 5 University Townships near major industrial/logistic corridors to host universities, skill centres, and R&D hubs
  • Girls' hostel in every district for female STEM students (funded through viability gap funding)
  • High-Powered 'Education to Employment & Enterprise' Standing Committee to align curricula with jobs
  • 3 new National Institutes of Pharmaceutical Education and Research (NIPER)
  • 1 new National Institute of Design (NID)
  • 4 telescope infrastructure facilities upgraded for astrophysics and astronomy education
  • Continued expansion of Atal Tinkering Labs (ATLs got a major budget boost in 2026)
  • Reduced TCS on overseas education remittances to ease financial burden for students going abroad
Source: educationforallinindia.com – School Education in Budget 2026

AI in Education: Labs, Curriculum & National Mission
Government push to teach Artificial Intelligence from school level

India is one of the first countries in the world to plan a mandatory AI curriculum for students starting from Class 3. The government sees AI literacy as a basic skill — as important as reading and maths.

IndiaAI Mission Launched March 2024

The Government of India launched the IndiaAI Mission on March 7, 2024, with a total planned outlay of approximately ₹10,372 crore over five years. This is one of the largest government investments in Artificial Intelligence in the country's history. The mission aims to:

  • Build high-end compute infrastructure (GPUs) for AI research and education
  • Establish 80 IndiaAI Labs nationwide as hubs for AI research and development
  • Set up 20 AI Curation Units across Central Ministries to build datasets
  • Support 25 deep-tech startups in the AI sector
  • Launch the FutureSkills pillar to expand AI education at school, college, and professional levels
  • Develop AI tools in Indian languages for inclusive education in rural areas
Source: aidigitalnews.com – IndiaAI Mission Review
AI CoE for Education ₹500 Crore Centre of Excellence

Announced in Budget 2025-26, a Centre of Excellence (CoE) in Artificial Intelligence for Education will be set up with an outlay of ₹500 crore. This will be the 4th AI CoE in India (the other three focus on agriculture, health, and sustainable cities). The CoE will:

  • Develop personalized learning platforms powered by AI
  • Build AI-driven assessment tools to evaluate student progress automatically
  • Create smart content generation tools and virtual learning environments
  • Focus on AI education in Indian languages, especially for rural and low-resource schools
  • Bridge the gap between academia and industry in AI education
Source: indiaai.gov.in – AI Centre of Excellence in Education
AI Curriculum Mandate AI from Class 3 onwards — 2026–27

In October 2025, the Department of School Education & Literacy (Ministry of Education) announced that AI and Computational Thinking (AI & CT) will be introduced as a mandatory subject in all schools starting from Class 3, beginning in the 2026–27 academic session. This is aligned with NEP 2020 and the National Curriculum Framework for School Education (NCF-SE) 2023.

  • Classes 3 to 8: Implementation in 2026–27
  • Classes 9 to 10: Follow-up in 2027–28
  • An expert committee chaired by Prof. Karthik Raman (IIT Madras) was formed by CBSE to design the curriculum
  • CBSE has already introduced AI as an elective from Class 6 (Subject Code 417 and 843)
  • CISCE (ICSE board) added robotics and AI to its curriculum from the 2025–26 year
  • Resource materials and digital handbooks to be ready by end of 2025
  • Teacher training under the NISHTHA programme will run alongside rollout
Source: pib.gov.in – AI Curriculum from Class 3 Onwards (Official PIB)
Existing AI Stats CBSE & Schools (as of 2025)
  • Over 8 lakh students from 4,500+ schools are already enrolled in AI courses under CBSE and other boards
  • CBSE collaborated with IBM to integrate AI curriculum in 200 schools across India
  • India's EdTech AI market projected to grow at 33.5% CAGR from 2024–2028
  • 62% of educators use generative AI for lesson planning and student feedback
Source: extramarks.com – AI in Education India 2025

Atal Tinkering Labs (ATLs) — Massive Expansion
Hands-on STEM labs for students with 3D printers, robotics kits, AI tools

Atal Tinkering Labs are physical STEM labs inside schools where students can experiment with robotics, 3D printers, electronics, AI kits, sensors, and more. Each lab receives ₹20 lakh to set up and ₹10 lakh/year to operate.

Budget 2025 Announcement 50,000 New ATLs Over 5 Years

In the Union Budget 2025-26, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman announced the establishment of 50,000 Atal Tinkering Labs in government schools over the next five years. This is a fivefold expansion from the existing 10,000 labs across 722 districts.

  • Current base (as of early 2025): 10,000 ATLs already operational across India
  • New target: 50,000 ATLs over next 5 years — reaching every district, town, and village
  • Budget for ATLs jumped 300%, from ₹96.83 crore to ₹400 crore
  • Each new lab will receive ₹20 lakh for setup and equipment
  • Labs will include 3D printers, sensors, robotics kits, AI tools, electronics, and coding equipment
  • Atal Incubation Centres in colleges will allow ATL students to continue innovation after school
  • Part of the broader Viksit Bharat @2047 vision to create innovation culture from grassroots
Source: businesstoday.in – Budget 2025: 50,000 Atal Tinkering Labs
2026 Update ATL Budget Gets Another Boost

In the Union Budget 2026-27, Atal Tinkering Labs received a significant further boost. The government continues to prioritize expanding STEM infrastructure in government schools. The Atal Innovation Mission (AIM) under NITI Aayog manages the ATL program.

Source: careers360.com – School Education Budget 2026
Confirmed (Dec 2025) Education Minister Reaffirms 50,000 ATL Target

In December 2025, Union Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan confirmed during Rajya Sabha's Question Hour that more than 10,000 ATLs are already operational, and the expansion to 50,000 is firmly underway. He said ATLs have created "a strong network of creativity, experimentation, and problem-solving" among India's youth.

Source: ibef.org – IBEF: 50,000 Atal Tinkering Labs Confirmed

PM SHRI Schools — Model Schools for NEP 2020
Pradhan Mantri Schools for Rising India — transforming existing schools
Overview What are PM SHRI Schools?

PM SHRI (Pradhan Mantri Schools for Rising India) is a centrally sponsored scheme launched on September 5, 2022 (Teachers' Day). It aims to upgrade over 14,500 existing government schools into model institutions that showcase NEP 2020 implementation. The scheme runs from 2022-23 to 2026-27 with a total budget of ₹27,360 crore.

Each PM SHRI school gets upgraded with: Smart Classrooms, Computer Labs, Integrated Science Labs, Atal Tinkering Labs, Vocational Labs, Digital Libraries, Green Initiatives (solar panels, water conservation), and modern sports facilities.

Source: dsel.education.gov.in – Official PM SHRI Portal
2024–2025 Progress 7,500+ Schools Upgraded
  • As of end of 2025, over 7,500 schools have been transformed into PM SHRI model schools
  • 12,079 schools have been selected from 32 States/UTs (out of 14,500 target) in 4 selection phases
  • 33 States/UTs and KVS/NVS have signed MoUs with the Ministry of Education
  • Budget for PM SHRI increased by ₹1,450 crore compared to the previous year in Budget 2025-26
  • The government is on track to meet its goal of 14,500 PM SHRI schools by 2027
Source: newscanvassedu.com – PM SHRI 2025 Milestones
Infrastructure in Each PM SHRI What Students Get
  • Smart Classrooms with digital boards, projectors, and connected internet
  • Integrated Science Labs with modern instruments and equipment
  • Computer Labs with up-to-date hardware and software
  • Atal Tinkering Labs for robotics, AI, and hands-on STEM activities
  • Vocational training labs aligned with NSQF (National Skills Qualification Framework)
  • Digital libraries with e-content access
  • BaalVatika (pre-primary learning zone) for foundational-stage children
  • Green/Eco-friendly campus features (solar energy, rainwater harvesting)
Source: drishtiias.com – PM SHRI Scheme Details

Digital Learning Infrastructure
Platforms, broadband, smart classrooms, and edtech tools for all students
DIKSHA Platform National Digital Education Portal

DIKSHA (Digital Infrastructure for Knowledge Sharing) is India's national school education platform. As of December 2024, DIKSHA hosts over 80,000 learning resources in 36 Indian languages, serves 50+ million users monthly, and has facilitated over 600 million learning sessions. It covers students from Class 1 to 12 across 35 states and UTs.

  • Operates as India's "One Nation, One Digital Platform" for school education
  • Integrated into the NDEAR (National Digital Education Architecture) framework
  • Teacher training modules on DIKSHA have reached over 4.2 million teachers
Source: ciet.ncert.gov.in – Official DIKSHA (NCERT)
SWAYAM Platform Free Online Courses (MOOCs)

SWAYAM offers over 2,000 Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) from Class 9 to post-graduate levels, completely free of cost. As of 2024, over 45 million learners are registered on SWAYAM and over 8.5 million certificates have been issued. Credit transfer integration allows students to earn academic credits through SWAYAM courses.

Source: vyyuha.com – Digital Education Initiatives India
BharatNet + Budget 2025 Broadband for Rural Schools

In Budget 2025-26, the government announced broadband connectivity to all government secondary schools in rural areas under the BharatNet project. This is a crucial step to bridge the digital divide and ensure rural students can access online learning tools, DIKSHA, SWAYAM, and AI-powered education platforms.

  • BharatNet Phase II aims to connect 2.5 lakh gram panchayats
  • As of 2024, only about 58% of schools have functional computer labs
  • Only 27% of students currently have consistent access to internet-enabled devices
Source: deccanherald.com – Budget 2025: Broadband for Rural Schools
NDEAR National Digital Education Architecture

NDEAR is India's unified digital education blueprint that connects all platforms (DIKSHA, SWAYAM, e-Pathshala, etc.) into one interoperable system. The Budget 2025-26 targets NDEAR and smart classroom expansion to 50% of government schools by 2026-27. The system allows states to build their own apps on top of NDEAR's open-source framework.

Source: buddy4study.com – NEP 2025 Digital Roadmap
Budget 2026 — New AVGC Content Creator Labs in 15,000 Schools

Budget 2026-27 announced a brand-new initiative: AVGC (Animation, Visual Effects, Gaming, Comics) Content Creator Labs to be set up in 15,000 secondary schools and 500 colleges. Backed by the Indian Institute of Creative Technologies (IICT), Mumbai, these labs will train students in digital creative skills to serve India's growing AVGC industry, which is projected to need 2 million professionals by 2030.

Source: organiser.org – Budget 2026: AVGC Labs & University Townships

NEP 2020 — Implementation Updates (2024–2026)
Key changes from the National Education Policy being rolled out across India
Structural Change New 5+3+3+4 School System

The NEP 2020's new school structure replaces the old 10+2 system with a 5+3+3+4 framework. CBSE began full rollout across its schools in 2025-26:

  • Foundational Stage (Age 3–8): 3 years pre-primary + Classes 1 & 2 — play-based learning
  • Preparatory Stage (Age 8–11): Classes 3 to 5 — activity-based learning
  • Middle Stage (Age 11–14): Classes 6 to 8 — project-based learning with subject introduction
  • Secondary Stage (Age 14–18): Classes 9 to 12 — flexible, multidisciplinary subject choices
  • The rigid stream separation (Arts/Science/Commerce) in Classes 11-12 is being dismantled
Source: cyberschoolmanager.com – NEP 2024-25 Explained
Language Policy Mother Tongue and Indian Languages

Under NEP 2020 implementation (2024 onwards), the medium of instruction up to Class 5 (and in some schools up to Class 8) must be in the student's mother tongue or a regional Indian language. For Classes 11-12, two languages are compulsory — at least one must be an Indian language. This aims to bridge learning gaps and preserve India's multilingual heritage.

Source: getmyuni.com – NEP Language and Reform Details
Curriculum Revisions NCERT Textbook Overhaul
  • NCERT is revising textbooks for Grades 7, 9, and 11 to focus on competency-based learning (completion by 2026)
  • No Detention Policy has been scrapped to address learning gaps and promote accountability
  • Board exams to test understanding rather than memory — objective + descriptive formats, biannual mode
  • Vocational education is now mandatory for Classes 6 to 8 as part of NEP rollout
  • Over 2 lakh students have enrolled in vocational courses since the NEP launched
Source: buddy4study.com – NEP 2025 Milestones and Challenges
Higher Education CUET, Credit Banks, Flexible Degrees
  • CUET (Common University Entrance Test) being implemented in 90% of central universities
  • Academic Bank of Credits allows students to earn credits from multiple institutions
  • SWAYAM integrated with credit transfer mechanisms for higher education
  • India's Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER) in higher education reached 32.5% in 2024; NEP targets 50% by 2035
  • India aims to have 25 universities in the global top 200 by 2030
Source: collegevidya.com – India Education System 2026

New Regulations, Rules & Government Directives
Policy mandates and regulatory changes in education (2024–2026)
Ministry of Education AI Curriculum — Official Directive (Oct 2025)

The Department of School Education & Literacy (DoSE&L) officially held a stakeholder consultation on October 29, 2025, bringing together CBSE, NCERT, KVS, NVS, and external experts to finalize India's AI & CT curriculum. This resulted in a government directive that AI education will be treated as a "basic universal skill" linked to "The World Around Us" (TWAU) — a new educational concept connecting AI learning to real-life contexts. This is a binding directive across CBSE, KVS, NVS, and state schools.

Source: pib.gov.in – Official PIB: AI CT Curriculum Directive
New Committee — 2026 Education to Employment & Enterprise Standing Committee

The Union Budget 2026-27 announced a High-Powered 'Education to Employment and Enterprise' Standing Committee. This committee will ensure that school and college curricula are continuously updated to match industry needs. One of its specific mandates is to assess the impact of AI on employment and recommend how to embed AI skills into education from the school level onwards.

Source: insightsonindia.com – Budget 2026 Education and Skill Reforms
DPDP Rules 2025 Data Protection in Digital Education

The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Rules, 2025, were notified on November 14, 2025. These rules apply to all digital education platforms including DIKSHA, SWAYAM, and school management systems. Platforms collecting student data must follow data minimization, consent, and security requirements — important for the growing AI and digital education ecosystem.

Source: aidigitalnews.com – DPDP Rules and IndiaAI Mission
MoU Requirement PM SHRI Scheme — State Compliance

All states and UTs must sign a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the Ministry of Education to participate in the PM SHRI scheme and receive funding. As of 2024-25, 33 States/UTs and KVS/NVS have signed the MoU. States that did not sign (such as Tamil Nadu, West Bengal) have had disagreements with the Centre, but most have now joined the scheme.

Source: dsel.education.gov.in – PM SHRI Official Page

Future Technology in Education — Government Push
Robotics, coding, AI labs, and technology skilling for students
IITs — 21st Century Courses AI, Robotics, Biotechnology

As announced in Budget 2025, IITs are being expanded and updated to offer 21st-century courses in Artificial Intelligence, Robotics, and Biotechnology. 6,500 new seats are being added to third-generation IITs over 5 years. The number of IIT students, which was 65,000 when PM Modi took office, is expected to more than double through this expansion.

Source: careers360.com – IIT Expansion and 21st Century Courses
SOAR & YUVAi AI Literacy Programmes for Students and Teachers

The IndiaAI Mission has launched specific programmes to build AI literacy at the grassroots level. SOAR and YUVAi are student and youth-focused programmes combining short-course modules, hackathons, and project-based learning to foster both technical AI skills and ethical understanding of AI. These programmes are being integrated with schools, colleges, and teacher training institutes.

Source: veloxxmedia.com – India's AI Education Push
5 National Centres of Excellence for Skilling (2025)

Announced in Budget 2025-26, five National Centres of Excellence for Skilling will be established in collaboration with global partners. These centres will support the "Make for India, Make for the World" manufacturing vision and will cover curriculum design, training of trainers, and skills certification. They will specifically focus on high-tech industries including AI, advanced manufacturing, and Industry 4.0 skills.

Source: thestatesman.com – Budget 2025-26 Skilling Centres
University Townships — 2026 5 New Academic Mega-Hubs

The Union Budget 2026-27 announced that the government will support states in creating 5 University Townships near major industrial and logistics corridors. These will be integrated academic hubs housing multiple universities, colleges, research institutions, skill centres, and residential complexes. The goal is to create a seamless pipeline from education to employment in India's fastest-growing industrial zones.

Source: thesouthfirst.com – Budget 2026: University Townships and Girls' Hostels
